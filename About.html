Our Story

Starbucks Corporation is an American multinational chain of coffeehouses and roastery reserves headquartered in Seattle, Washington.
As the world's largest coffeehouse chain, Starbucks is seen to be the main representation of the United States' third wave of coffee culture.
As of early 2024, the company operates over 32,000 locations worldwide.

History

Starbucks Corporation is an American multinational chain of coffeehouses and roastery reserves headquartered in Seattle, Washington.
As of early 2024, it operates over 35,000 locations worldwide.

Starbucks was founded in Seattle, Washington, in 1971 by Jerry Baldwin, Zev Siegl, and Gordon Bowker.
The company started as a single store selling high-quality coffee beans and equipment.
It has since grown into one of the largest coffeehouse chains in the world.

Sustainability

Starbucks is committed to sustainability and social responsibility. The company has initiatives in place to reduce its environmental impact, including:

  • Offering reusable cups and encouraging customers to bring their own
  • Investing in renewable energy sources
  • Supporting coffee farmers through ethical sourcing practices
  • Reducing waste through recycling and composting programs
